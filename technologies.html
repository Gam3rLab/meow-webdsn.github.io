Solar Energy Technologies

Photovoltaic(PV) Solar Panels

The PV solar panel is built from multiple layers, an layer of tempered glass to protect it from weather conditions, an aluminum frame to house the unit, plastic polymers to provide insulation, and a layer of silicon semiconductor cells.

The cells have a postive and negative layer, which creates an internal electric field. When photons from sunlight hit the silicon, they knock electrons loose from their atoms and the internal electric field pushes the free electrons to flow in a single direction, where it is collected by metal wiring as energy.

Solar Thermal Collectors

A solar thermal collector converts sunlight into usable heat, it consists of 5 components that are encased in a protective outer casing.

Firstly, sunlight passes through a transparent top cover that traps heat inside. The light then hits a black coated metal absorber plate, converting solar radiation into thermal energy. The thermal energy is then conducted into metal tubes with flowing water or glycol which is a good heat conductor. The casing is also covered in insulation that prevents the heat from escaping, forcing the hot fluid to travel down to a storage tank where an heat exchanger transfers the thermal energy to your water supply before pumping the used fluid back up to repeat the cycle.

Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) Systems

A Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) system uses mirrors or lenses to focus sunlight onto a receiver.

The reflected light hits a receiver, such as a pipe with specialized heat transfer fluids like molten salt, generating high-temperatures to heat up the liquid. The liquid is then passed through pipes to heat up water and create steam. The steam then drives a turbine to generate kinetic electricity.

Along with that, the thermal energy can also be stored in the fluid for later use, letting CSP plants produce power after the sun has set.
